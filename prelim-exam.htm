Introduction

Importance Of this SDG

Health is important because it is the foundation of life. If a person is healthy, they can study, work, play, and fulfill their responsibilities in the community. This SDG is important because its goal is to Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages. With this goal, we can build a lively and productive community without fear of sickness. Good health allows people to reach their full potential and contribute to the development of society. Poor health, on the other hand, can lead to suffering, limit opportunities, and cause destruction in families and communities. By promoting health and well-being for everyone, we are also securing a brighter, stronger, and more sustainable future for all.

Current Situatuion

The current situation of this SDG shows progress, but the progress is still too slow to achieve the 2030 goals. First, maternal mortality has decreased since year 2000, but the reduction is not fast enough to meet the target. Child and newborn mortality rates have also improved greatly, yet more effort is needed to save millions of young lives. HIV-related deaths have decreased as well, but there is still a high number of new positive cases being reported in several regions. In addition, neglected tropical diseases continue to affect around 1.5 billion people worldwide, especially in poor communities. To address these challenges, the United Nations is working to achieve universal health coverage, strengthen pandemic preparedness, and improve mental health care, while also focusing on the most vulnerable and underserved populations. This shows that while there has been progress, stronger global action and commitment are needed to fully achieve SDG 3 by 2030.

Practical actions individuals can take to support the SDG
  1. Modern people can share awareness, especially through social media, and promote health programs.
    • By posting and sharing reliable health information online, individuals can reach more people and encourage them to practice healthy lifestyles.

  2. Promote mental health awareness and educate others about important health issues.
    • Talking about mental health openly helps reduce stigma, supports those who are struggling, and reminds people that mental well-being is just as
      important as physical health.

  3. Get involved in vaccinations and have regular check-ups.
    • Vaccinations protect not only yourself but also the community, while regular check-ups help detect illnesses early and keep you healthy.

  4. Practice healthy habits such as eating nutritious food, exercising, and getting enough rest.
    • Good habits strengthen the body, boost the immune system, and allow individuals to live more productive lives.

  5. Maintain proper hygiene and sanitation to prevent the spread of diseases.
    • Simple actions like handwashing, cleaning surroundings, and using safe water can stop infections and protect community health.
